About Me

I'm an undergraduate researcher at the University of Pennsylvania's School of Engineering and Applied Science (SEAS), where I'm diving deep into the world of Artificial Intelligence. Currently, I'm working on AI with fantastical narrative under the guidance of Professor Callison-Burch.

My academic journey is driven by a passion for understanding the intersection of AI, mathematics, and linguistics. I'm particularly interested in how these fields can inform and enhance each other, leading to more sophisticated and human-like AI systems.

Languages: French, Hindi, Sanskrit; learning Spanish and Chinese.

Research & Interests

Artificial Intelligence

My research focuses on developing interactive AI systems and exploring human-AI interaction. I am particularly interested in how artificial intelligence can be designed to not only solve complex problems but also to communicate and collaborate with humans in meaningful ways. My work involves building systems that can learn from and adapt to user feedback, making AI more accessible and intuitive. Check out my AI Pong Game demonstration below!

Mathematics & Linguistics

I explore the mathematical foundations of AI and their applications in language processing. My work in this area draws from my studies in Sanskrit, where I analyze the structure and logic of ancient texts to inform modern computational models. I am also looking to do work in mathematical linguistics, focusing on how formal systems and logic can be used to better understand language and meaning. This interdisciplinary approach allows me to bridge the gap between abstract theory and practical application in AI.

Computational Biology

I'm passionate about applying computational methods to solve biological problems. My work in this area combines machine learning with biological data analysis to uncover new insights. I am especially interested in how algorithms can be used to model complex biological systems, from genetic networks to protein folding. By integrating computational tools with experimental data, I aim to contribute to advances in personalized medicine and our understanding of life at the molecular level.
